Syntax

The subsequent instructions will explain the proper syntax for searching for a specific student of group of students.

Checking for blanks

Enter the word 'NULL' if you want to search for blanks or missing data. Conversely, if you are interested in fields that are NOT blank, then you must enter 'NOT NULL'.

OR Syntax

Commas translate into OR for fields.
For example: Say you were interested in students that have been going to class for 4 OR 5 years. Just enter: 4,5 in the input field under 'Year'.

LIKE Syntax

For those of us who don't speak SQL, LIKE is equivalent to 'contains' in spoken English. For example: You need to find info on a person who goes by 'Brad', and his last name starts with a 'Z'. Not everyone who goes by 'Brad' is named 'Brad', but 'Bradley', etc. The 'Z' first letter should reduce most of the Brad's out there.
So, this is achieved by: Brad% for the first name, and Z% for the last name. You're telling the database to search for everyone with a first name starts with 'Brad' and a last name starts with 'Z'.
