Introduction

Marking courses as unwanted allows you to prevent them from being created in Moodle. This benefits those who do not want to confuse their students into thinking the Moodle course will be used by the instructor to teach his/her course. If your course has already been created in Moodle, then marking it as unwanted will unenroll all students and instructors including yourself; the Moodle course will be unavailable until you decide to teach it in Moodle. Unwanted course preferences will be processed within an hour of being requested.

Note: If you have the default delete option set in Course Creation and Enrollment, then the course will be deleted when enrollment is zero, or more precisely, when an entire course is unwanted.

How to mark a section as unwanted

Marking specific sections as being unwanted allows you to decide exactly which courses should be taught in Moodle. In order to mark specific sections as unwanted, click the checkboxes beside the sections as highlighted below and click the "Save changes" button at the bottom of the page. Once the page reloads, a message should appear stating that your changes were saved and the checkboxes besides the sections you selected will be checked.

How to mark all sections as unwanted

Marking all sections of a course as being unwanted prevents all of your current and future sections of the course from being taught in Moodle. In order to mark specific sections as unwanted, click the "All" below the course name as highlighted below and click the "Save changes" button at the bottom of the page. Once the page reloads, a message should appear stating that your changes were saved and the checkboxes you selected will be checked.
